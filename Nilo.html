The first black president from Brazil

 
 

Nilo Peçanha (1867-1924) was a Brazilian politician, known for being the seventh president of Brazil, serving from 1909 to 1910. He assumed the presidency after the death of then president Afonso Pena. Before that, Peçanha had been vice-president, governor of the state of Rio de Janeiro and federal deputy. Peçanha stood out for being one of the first presidents of mixed race origin, at a time when racism was very present in Brazilian society. His government was marked by efforts to modernize the country and support education, including the creation of the Indian Protection Service (SPI) and the founding of technical schools. After his presidency, he continued to be active politically, serving as a senator and minister of foreign affairs. Nilo Peçanha is remembered as a progressive leader who sought to implement important reforms in several areas of public administration.
