The first person in Brazil

 
 

The Bachelor of Cananeia is a historical figure associated with the beginning of Portuguese colonization in Brazil. His real name is Cosme Fernandes, and he was a Portuguese convict who settled in the Cananeia region, on the south coast of the state of São Paulo, at the beginning of the 16th century. Cosme Fernandes was sent to Brazil as part of an exile policy, where criminals were exiled to overseas colonies as a form of punishment. He arrived in the Cananeia region probably around 1502. He became an influential figure in the region, establishing himself as a local leader and trader, interacting with both indigenous people and other European colonizers. The Bachelor of Cananeia is a figure shrouded in mystery and legend, but his role is recognized as significant in the early years of the Portuguese presence in South America.
