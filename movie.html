Better than Chaplin

 
 

On June 19th, Brazilian Cinema Day is celebrated, a date that has as reference the first cinematographic recordings made in Brazil. Brazilian Cinema Day is celebrated on June 19th. This date was chosen because it refers to what would have been the first day on which images were made using cinematograph technology in Brazil, that is: June 19, 1898. Such images were recorded on board the ship Brésil, which had leaving Boudeaux, in France, where the Italian Afonso Segreto had just taken a course on cinematograph operation and, from there, ended up bringing one of the pieces of equipment to Brazil. The images captured the scenery of Guanabara Bay, in Rio de Janeiro.
